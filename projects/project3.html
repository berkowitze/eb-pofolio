Six Issues Of The Hornbill Monthly & Other Evidence Of A Crime

An experimental novel set in the South African hinterland.

process

conceptualization -> writing -> editing -> typography & layout -> visual critique sessions -> asset production -> print proofing -> ...

early readers

Steven Hagerty, Rachel Kaplan, Vivian Strauss, Marina Stevenson, Amanda Bent

duration

8 years and counting

Problem Statement

Eight years ago, I spent three months working as a wildlife safari guide in a wilderness community in South Africa. During my time there, I met a number of people whose kindness blew me away even as their flagrant racism and heinous actions unsettled me. Those three months were a formative time for me, but I've always struggled to tell the story for fear of mischaracterizing it. My perspective on the events is as valid as anyone else's. Might there be a way to make the story a little less biased?

Outcome

Six Issues Of The Hornbill Monthly & Other Evidence Of A Crime is a work of fiction. The meat of the novel is six consecutive issues of a monthly magazine published by the residents of Hornbill Reserve, a gated community abutting Kruger National Park, in South Africa. The novel begins when its protagonist, Neil—queer, brown, idealistic, young—flies in from America to join the community at Hornbill, and it ends when Neil leaves South Africa six months later, following a destructive fire that sweeps through the reserve. Upon his departure, the magazines, along with other objects in his possession, become a deconstructed narrative of what happened. The book contains printed articles, hand-written diary entries, illustrations, photographs, and advertisements. Besides Neil, other notable characters in the book include an ecologist/assassin named Conrad, a hotelier/trainwreck named Jenna, a zebra named Nwana, and, most telling of all, a monkey named Obama.

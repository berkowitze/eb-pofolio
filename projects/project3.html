Summary

Six Issues Of The Hornbill Monthly & Other Evidence Of A Crime is a work of fiction. The meat of the novel is six consecutive issues of a monthly magazine published by the residents of Hornbill Reserve, a gated community abutting Kruger National Park, in South Africa. The novel begins when its protagonist, Arjun, moves there with his famous-writer partner, Ujo, who is there for a year-long artist's residency. The couple are charmed by the community at first, but as time goes by, they begin to discover its oddities and, ultimately, its unforgivable perversities. Their time at Hornbill comes to an end following a destructive fire that sweeps through the reserve. Upon Arjun's departure, the magazines, along with other objects in his possession, become a deconstructed narrative of what happened. The book contains printed articles, hand-written diary entries, illustrations, photographs, and advertisements. Besides Arjun and Ujo, other notable characters in the book include an ecologist/assassin named Conrad, a hotelier/trainwreck named Jenna, a zebra named Nwana, and a monkey named Obama.
